### Category: Educational Series

**Post L36 — Series: Cloud Basics for African Business #1**
What is cloud storage, really?

Cloud storage = someone else's computer, accessed over the internet.

When you upload a file to Google Drive, it travels from your device → your ISP → undersea cable → US data center → Google's hard drives.

That file is now subject to:
- US law (CLOUD Act)
- Google's terms of service
- Google's security practices
- Monthly payment to Google

Is that how you want your business documents stored?

---

**Post L37 — Series: Cloud Basics #2**
What is private cloud storage?

Private cloud = YOUR computer(s), organized and accessible like a cloud service.

When you upload a file to VaultaCloud (self-hosted):
→ File travels from your device to your server (on LAN: instant)
→ Your server (in your office or on a VPS you control)
→ Subject to Nigerian law, your terms, your security

File never leaves your jurisdiction unless you choose to sync it somewhere.

---

**Post L38 — Series: Cloud Basics #3**
What is offline-first architecture?

Most cloud storage: you → internet → file (internet required)
Offline-first: file already on your device → changes sync to server

The difference:
• Online-first: no internet = no files
• Offline-first: no internet = still works, syncs later

For African markets with intermittent connectivity, this isn't a detail. It's the architecture that keeps businesses running.

---

**Post L39 — Series: Cloud Basics #4**
LAN deployment — what it is and why it matters:

LAN = Local Area Network = your office's internal network

When VaultaCloud is deployed on your LAN:
→ Files access at 100–1000 Mbps (not 5–50 Mbps internet speed)
→ No international bandwidth consumed (saves money)
→ Zero latency (server is 10 meters away, not 10,000 km)
→ Works during full internet outage

LAN-first is how offices with serious file storage have operated for 30 years. VaultaCloud brings it to African SMEs without an enterprise IT team.
