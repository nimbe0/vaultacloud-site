## Nigerian Startup VaultaCloud Launches Private Cloud Storage Built for Africa's Infrastructure Reality

*Abuja-based platform offers offline-first file management with one-time pricing — no monthly subscriptions, no US server dependency*

**ABUJA, NIGERIA — January 2025** — VaultaCloud, a private cloud storage and file management platform built for African businesses and emerging markets, today announced the public launch of its product. The platform is designed to solve a problem that mainstream cloud storage vendors have ignored: building file infrastructure that works reliably in markets with intermittent internet connectivity, expensive bandwidth, and the need to keep data on local infrastructure.

"Google Drive, Dropbox, and OneDrive were designed for markets with always-on internet and cheap bandwidth," said Nimbe Oduware, founder of VaultaCloud. "Nigerian and African businesses have been trying to fit their operations into tools that don't fit their environment. VaultaCloud is built for our reality — it works without internet, stores data on infrastructure you own, and costs you once, not every month in US dollars."

**The Problem**

Cloud storage tools from major US technology companies assume a connectivity environment that doesn't exist in much of Africa. When internet service is disrupted — due to power outages, ISP failures, or bandwidth constraints — these tools become inaccessible, halting workflows for legal firms accessing case files, accountants reviewing financial statements, architects sharing project drawings, and virtually any knowledge worker who relies on cloud storage.

Additionally, major cloud providers store data on servers subject to US legal jurisdiction, creating potential compliance concerns under Nigeria's NDPA, Kenya's DPA, and other African data protection frameworks. And their per-seat monthly pricing in US dollars creates ongoing foreign exchange exposure for local businesses.

**The Solution**

VaultaCloud deploys on a business's own server — either in their office, on their local area network (LAN), or on a cloud virtual private server (VPS) they control. Files are cached locally for offline access and sync automatically when connectivity is available.

Key features include:
- Offline-first file access — works through internet outages
- LAN deployment — sub-second file access on office networks
- Role-based team permissions and department workspaces
- Complete activity audit log
- Secure file sharing with expiring links
- White-label deployment for IT consultants and resellers
- WhatsApp integration for file sharing
- One-time purchase pricing: Starter ($299), Business ($699), Enterprise (custom)

**Target Market**

VaultaCloud targets Nigerian and African businesses across sectors including law, architecture and engineering, accounting, healthcare, education, real estate, media, and government. The platform is also positioned for IT consultants across Africa who want to offer private cloud storage as a managed service under their own brand.

**About VaultaCloud**

VaultaCloud is a private cloud storage and file management platform headquartered in Abuja, Nigeria. The company was founded with the mission of giving every African organization sovereign control over their files through infrastructure designed for Africa's connectivity reality.

## VaultaCloud Launches Reseller Program for African IT Consultants

*White-label licensing enables IT professionals to offer branded private cloud storage to clients*

**ABUJA, NIGERIA** — VaultaCloud today announced the launch of its reseller and white-label licensing program, enabling IT consultants and managed service providers across Africa to deploy VaultaCloud's private cloud storage platform under their own brand.

Under the program, IT professionals license VaultaCloud and deploy it for their clients with custom branding, their own domain (e.g., files.clientname.com), and their own company identity. Clients access professional private cloud storage without knowing VaultaCloud powers the infrastructure.

"African IT consultants are already trusted by their clients for technology decisions," said VaultaCloud founder Nimbe Oduware. "We're giving them a product that creates recurring managed service revenue — something most consultants in our market struggle to build. Deploy private cloud for a client, charge a management fee, and create an ongoing relationship rather than a one-off project."

The reseller program offers volume licensing discounts for consultants deploying for multiple clients, full technical documentation for deployment and management, and priority technical support.

Interested IT consultants can contact reseller@vaultacloud.com for program details.
